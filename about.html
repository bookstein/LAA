AboutOur Practice, Insurance, & Staff

Our Practice

Ever since Avery and Associates Physical Therapy opened for practice in 1989, our goal has been to help patients recover their quality of life.

We offer a full range of physical therapy services, using a variety of tools and hands-on techniques to help patients regain whole-body health. Our physical therapists collectively have decades of experience working with people of all backgrounds and conditions. We also have special expertise in physical therapy for orthopedics, neurology, rehabilitation, and women's health. For all patients, we emphasize holistic treatments that promote healing and long-term well-being.

Every patient seen in our office receives a 1-hour, comprehensive initial evaluation on their first visit. Afterwards, we follow up with patients to check on progress and to help further reduce or eliminate pain. These follow-up treatments are typically 45 minutes long, and are always one-on-one and private. Sessions are individualized for each patient's needs.

It’s very important to us that patients feel empowered throughout the healing process. To this end, we educate patients about their conditions, and teach self-care methods, home exercises, and self-help techniques — including stress management, posture, and muscle control.

Women’s health is also an important part of our practice. We have experience addressing issues throughout women’s life cycle, including before and after pregnancy and during menopause.

Staff

    Linda Avery- Physical Therapist -

    Linda received her physical therapy certificate in 1965 from the UCSF Medical Center. Her specialties include orthopedic, spinal, and obstretical-gynological problems. She has also been certified in Lamaze childbirth education and biofeedback. Linda helped develop one of the first programs in the US for high risk maternity patients. Her experience in childbirth education has since evolved into a specialization in women's health and then pelvic health for both men and women.

    Over her career, Linda has focused on learning and developing manual therapy skills in order to address the body's multiple systems, including the musculo-skeletal, neurovascular, and autonomic nervous systems. Her philosophy is to consider the whole person, not only injured or painful parts. “I love my work,” she says. “I am proud of the quality of care and expertise I can offer my patients.”

    Linda founded Avery and Associates Physical Therapy in 1989.

    Tracy Trumbly - Physical Therapist -

    Tracy graduated from the Chapman University and Children's Hospital of Los Angeles physical therapy program in 1987. She has worked in outpatient clinics as a physical therpist for the last 26 years. Her expertise includes hand therapy, spine and orthopedic work, neuromuscular facilitation, repetitive strain injuries, and women's health/pelvic floor issues. She is also a certified feldenkrais practitioner.

    Tracy is specifically interested in geriatric physical therapy; her goal is to improve her patients' functionality, strength, and activity. In fact, her curiosity about how the body functions led her to pursue physical therapy in the first place. She explains, “I want to know what makes us work our best, how to move better and be more comfortable.”

    Britt Van Hees - Doctor of Physical Therapy -

    Britt received her doctorate in physical therapy at Samuel Merritt University in Oakland. She has significant experience in pelvic floor work and women's health, functional manual therapy, and Janda muscle imbalance theory. Through individualized treatment and patient education, Britt seeks to empower patients to maintain their own rehabilitation process. “Education is a big part of physical therapy for me,” she says. “You can't have true change without understanding.”

    A certified pilates instructor, Britt teaches classes at Precision Point Pilates in Oakland. She is also pursuing further education in women's health with Herman & Wallace Pelvic Rehabilitation Institute starting in fall 2014.

    Anne Aronov - Certified Massage Therapist -

    Anne graduated from Bennington College in 1979 with a degree in Dance and Psychology. She also holds a Master's Degree in Education from Lesley College in Massachusetts. Anne began practicing massage in 1981, while living in Israel. Trained in Shiatsu at the Ohashi Institute in New York City and the Acupressure Institute in Berkeley, her style is a mix of Shiatsu, Acupressure, Deep Tissue, Lomi Somatic Work and Lymphatic Drainage Massage. Her bodywork is also informed by years as a dancer and movement teacher.

    Anne has extensive experience with injury-related bodywork and has worked with people of all ages and physical conditions.

    Monique Green- Office Manager -

    Monique has been the office manager at Avery & Associates Physical Therapy since 2009. Having previously worked as an account manager, credentialing specialist, and utilization management, she brings over a decade of management and administrative experience to Avery & Associates.
